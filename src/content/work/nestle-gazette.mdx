## The outcome

*Gazette* is Nestlé's internal video newsletter — leadership interviews and zone-level distribution. The most recent edition featuring the CEO and Chairman of the Board reached 20,000 internal views in two weeks, the highest internal video engagement to date. Adventurous produces the editorial, video, and post-production pipeline.

## The operating problem

Internal communications at Nestlé runs at the scale of a public broadcaster: hundreds of thousands of employees, more than 80 countries, four operating zones, multiple working languages. Volume isn't the problem. Coherence is. Each zone runs its own internal channels with its own editorial logic; headquarters ships executive content into a fragmented signal environment where local cadence varies by region and by leader.

## The architecture

We built the editorial template for *Gazette* as a multi-zone instrument:

- A single executive interview format that translates across regions
- A production rhythm slow enough to clear corporate review and fast enough to land while the topic still matters
- A video-first format that reads in any language without lengthy localisation
- Editorial standards held by a small central team; distribution via the existing zone channels

The format itself is unremarkable on paper: longform interview, considered cuts, sober video grade. The architecture is what makes it consistent — and what makes the next leadership transition easier, not harder.

## The constraint we worked around

Nestlé is operating through one of the most significant leadership transitions in its modern history: new CEO, new Chairman, multiple senior departures, structural reorganisation. The *Gazette* format has continued through all of it, because the architecture sits below the personalities. The editorial calendar updates; the system holds.

## The next question

After a year of consistent production, the next question is whether internal executive content can do work beyond visibility — specifically, whether the *Gazette* format can be tuned for change communications during transitions. That's the engagement now in scope.
